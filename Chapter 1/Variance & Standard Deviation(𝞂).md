## For Population
Variance $= \sigma^2 = \frac{1}{N}\Sigma(x_{i}-\mu)^2$, $x_{i}$ represents each value, $\mu$ represents the mean, and $N$ represents the population size. 
Standard Deviation $= \sigma = \sqrt{ \frac{1}{N} \Sigma(X_{i}-\mu)^2 }$

## For Sample
Variance $= \sigma^2 = \frac{1}{{\color{Red}N-1}}\Sigma(x_{i}-\mu)^2$
Standard Deviation $= \sigma = \sqrt{ \frac{1}{{\color{Red}N-1}} \Sigma(X_{i}-\mu)^2 }$
