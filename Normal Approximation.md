When appropriate, use the Normal approximation to the <u>binomial distribution</u> to calculate probabilities.
$$
\displaylines{
\begin{align}
& \mathbf{1. Binomial} \\
& X ～ B(n,p) \\
& np ≥ 10,~n(1-p)≥10 \\
& \\
& \mathbf{2. Sample~Proportion} \\
& X ～ N\left( p, \sqrt{ \frac{{p(1-p)}}{n} } \right) \\
& n ≤ 10\%N \\
& np ≥ 10,~n(1-p) ≥ 10 \\
& \\
& \mathbf{3. Sample~Mean} \\
& X ～ N\left( \mu, \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{ n }} \right) \\
& 30 ≤ n ≤ 10\%N \\
& \\
& \mathbf{4. Poisson:X ～ Poi(\lambda)} \\
& X ～ N(\lambda, \sqrt{ \lambda }) \\
& \lambda ≥ 20
\end{align}
}
$$
